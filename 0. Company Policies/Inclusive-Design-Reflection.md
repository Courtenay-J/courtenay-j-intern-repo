# Includive Design

## Reflection
###  How can you adjust your communication style to be more inclusive of neurodivergent users and teammates?
- Assume good faith: everyone communicates differently, and sometimes things that are perceived as rude or blunt were intended to be benign
- Use clear, straightforward language.
- Use responsible terminology when discussing a vulnerable group. Saying "reduce ableist language" feels wrong, because I already make an active effort not to use such terms. But it never hurts to check what terminology a group uses for themselves.

### What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?
- Controls and/or their positions changing between screens: forces users to memorise the app's layout rather than being able to navigate it intuitively
- Controls that are too small: users with motor issues may be unable to click buttons, individuals with poor eyesight may not be able to read them, etc.
- Inconsistent or bright colour schemes: may cause sensory overload
  - Overanimation or flashing lights are overwhelming and may trigger epilepsy
- Difficult to understand language, or an overreliance on text
- Difficult to read fonts: people with dyslexia may additionally benefit from font settings

### What is one practical change you can make in your work to better support vulnerable populations?
Use less words. I have a bad habit of rambling, both in text and in person, and it overwhelms some people.

I'd also be interested in a large font setting for the windows app (it's straining my eyes), though I'm not sure if that falls within the scope of the frontend team.


## Task
### Identify one design or communication improvement that could make Focus Bear more accessible.
Some of Focus Bear (the app)'s language is difficult to read. For example, if pirate mode is accidentally enabled during the tutorial, it cannot be turned off until the user finishes it and manages to find the settings menu - which is also hidden in piratespeak. The tutorial would benefit from earlier options to turn it off, and key instructions being presented in plain, clear English.

Some buttons in the Android app are very small and hard to click: e.g. the "Add Manual Session" and "Focus Block Summary" buttons. Increasing the text size would make them easier for those with eyesight or motor issues.

Iconography would also serve to make the stats page more readable. The settings page uses icons to highlight what each button does - perhaps the same could be done for the Stats page.
