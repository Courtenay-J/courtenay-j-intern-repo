## How I Work Best
- I do my best work when I have clear instructions and a quiet environment.

- I prefer messages to be short and direct, and ideally via direct message rather than email. I don't mind "casual" language.

- Please do not contact me about work on social media or my personal Discord account.

## Collaboration & Teamwork
- I work best in a team when I have clear instructions/a clear goal, strong starting point, and expectations for when it should be finished.
- My biggest pet peeve in teamwork is people staying quiet when they are confused, and improper delegation of tasks. (Also, people running my work through Generative AI without asking first.)
- If I seem quiet in a meeting, it usually means I'm taking notes. I may also have a migraine - they are painless, but affect my focus and sometimes I'm not aware my concentration is waning until after the fact.

## Focus & Productivity
- I stay focused by working during scheduled hours and using the Focus Bear app to discourage scrolling. During meetings, I take notes to ensure I'm both listening and will remember key details.
- Things that distract me include:
  - Social media / browser games: Unfortunately I am a fan of pressing buttons.
  - Forgetting to eat: I have a bad habit of getting too focused on a task and not moving.
  - Excess noise / conversations: I may put headphones in or gently ask to continue a conversation later if I am working. 
  - People looking over my shoulder: Unless I've asked you to, of course.
- The best way to support my productivity is:
  - Give me time to process: I have limited office experience and can take time to "switch gears".
  - Give me to-do lists: I work well with clear instructions!
  - Let me take notes: my memory is not good.
  - Don't make small talk while I'm working: Nothing personal - I just need quiet to focus.
  - Regular check-ins: Do let me know how you're going with your stuff, and ask me where I'm at.

## Feedback & Growth
- I like feedback to be direct and private, preferably with examples. (While I am uncomfortable watching people read my work in front of me, please don't take this as a sign I am uninterested or upset!) Written feedback is preferred, but if verbal, please give me time to take notes.
- When I make a mistake, the best way to let me know is to send me a quick message (or tap me on the shoulder if we're in the same room). Again, please give me a chance to take notes.
- I appreciate it when teammates:
  - Check in on my progress. No offense is ever taken!
  - Let me know when they're struggling. Honesty's the best policy.
  - Let me know when I'm doing the right thing, just as much as the wrong thing. A total feedback void leaves me questioning.

If asked a question about logistics, I sometimes go blank/silent as I try to picture how I can fit it in to my plan. This is not equivalent to me saying no, nor am I offended. I'm mentally moving things around.
- A fun fact about me is I am an artist, and a very visual thinker. Drawing in the margins of my notes helps me visualise concepts. Even my "irrelevant" doodles are useful - they help me remember which page has what notes! 
- My work superpower is finding odd edge cases in UX tests that completely break everything. (When you work in game development, you learn to anticipate the most bizarre user behaviours.)
