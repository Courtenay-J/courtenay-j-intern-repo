# Professionalism Reflection
## Reflection
###  Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?
Working at McDonalds was a job of perpetual crunch: tight deadlines, constant backlog, highly variable environment. Uncaught mistakes would dominoe into worse problems, and working during the lunch rush during any sort of promotion made everything about ten times worse. Fortunately, I happened to be part of a very good team.

News had to travel fast. Managers would be informed of the day's promotions and have the kitchen prepare items in advance, cashiers would alternate between taking orders, managing the fries and packing orders. If anything broke or started to run low, it'd have to be shared with the rest of the crew so it could be fixed without customers noticing a delay. If there was a lull in the action, everyone would tidy the kitchen area to prevent future issues.

Finally, you also had to remember that the customers were often hungry, tired, or stressed/frustrated about the wait for their food or the MyMaccas app. It was important to never take it personally.

In short: pre-planning, quick and concise team communication, the ability to shift tasks depending on circumstances, and maintaining a professional demeanor.


### What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?
- Hold conversations in good faith: assume feedback is being given with your/the project's best interests in mind, and don't take feedback personally
- Use "I" statements to minimise unintentionally aggressive statements, and focus on critiquing the work rather than the person.
- Be concise: prepare what you have to say for meetings, etc. so you can get straight into it
- Use professional language (no swearing, insults, etc. Not that I would anyway, but it deserves a dot point.)

### How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?
- Be polite - see concise communication above
- Be efficient
- I feel this question overlaps with a lot of the above to be honest

### How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?
- Make note of what you've done; summarise it as concisely as possible
- Identify personal blockers in advance so you can inform team and get help
- Do your homework ahead of time, so you can explain what you've already tried and what you're still stuck on

### What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?
- Use the Focus Bear app, identify issues and give honest feedback 
- Suggest ideas for improvements
- Use the Discord to collaborate with others/share information
- Ask questions if unsure of something
- Ask for help/admit if stuck on something

### How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?
- Use emails for general, lower-priority questions (expected turnaround of about 48 hours)
- Use Discord or Teams for medium-priority questions (i.e. a response needed within 24 hours). This is also a good place to share information + coordinate with other interns
- SMS for an urgent issue that needs a quick response (~2 hours)
- Phonecall for emergencies that require an immediate response
- Concerns about professional conduct should be directed to HR
- In extreme circumstances (major concerns about professional conduct), contact Jeremy himself

### What are some things you won't do?
- Send emails with uninformative subject lines
- Rely on only one form of communication while things are getting bad
- Go ghost/stop responding to messages/updating the team on what's happening with your part of the project
- Wait to be told what to do
- Work off assumptions
- Struggle in silence without admitting help is needed
- Be passive in meetings 
- Keep ideas to self
