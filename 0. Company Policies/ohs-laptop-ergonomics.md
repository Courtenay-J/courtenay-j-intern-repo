# OHS Laptop Ergonomics

## Reflection
### What equipment changes can you make to improve your workspace setup?

My current desk setup is already somewhat ergonomic. I have an elbow rest to reduce shoulder strain on my right side, a wireless keyboard and mouse, and a laptop stand that raises my monitor up and keeps my neck straight. My desk is electric and can be raised to a standing height if need be, and I have a wobbleboard to get a little bit of extra muscle strength in. I do need a better office chair, but that might be out of budget right now. At least this one lets my feet stay flat on the floor and keeps my back straight.

However, there's... definitely room for improvement. I think I'd benefit from a second elbow rest for my left side. I currently lack a proper wrist rest, causing my arms to drag a bit while typing. A folded up towel might sort that up.

There's clutter here that prevents me from using the full desk space and I can feel myself leaning to compensate. And my monitor should really be higher up, or replaced with an external monitor. (I think I've got a curved one in storage that's designed to reduce both eye and neck strain.)

Working at Swinburne's late lab has its own issues, as the tables and chairs a very inconvenient height and it would weigh too much to bring proper equipment in. I may need to prop my laptop up with books and take more breaks.

### What behavioural changes can you implement to improve posture and reduce strain?

Taking a few minutes to clear my workspace/check it's properly set up before I start work for the day, so clutter doesn't pile up and I don't wind up working around physical blockages.

I focus far too strongly on a task and frequently forget to move for hours, so developing a proper break routine is task number one. I also forget my desk is a standing desk; I should clear the blockages preventing my desk from reaching full standing height and use that intermittently throughout the day.

I should stop crossing my legs while seated on this chair, too. It's far too easy to do.

Once I'm done typing things up, I'll also get rid of this stack of books that's wedged next to my mouse and properly recenter my workspace.

### How can you remind yourself to maintain good posture and take breaks throughout the day?

In the past I've used phone or browser timers to remind me to get up and move, but it's easy for me to not hear it or accidentally close the tab. So I think I'll give Focus Bear's movement break reminders a test.

In terms of maintaining good posture, though, it may be easier to set up a more regular secondary timer. A simple 'beep' every couple of minutes to remind me to check my posture, uncross my legs, etc. I'll test this out with [youtube videos](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TLfpNPqflm8) first, and if I like it I'll try a physical egg timer.

### Try using posture and movement reminders for a full workday and note any improvements.

Wow, yeah. My spine hurts way less when I'm not working at a diagonal angle for 4+ hours straight. Wrists are less strained with a towel under them. Timer video helped with posture for a while, until the video ran out without me noticing.

Not totally sure I'm using Focus Bear's timer correctly, and there have been times that I've ignored when it's told me to take a break. Perhaps I've missed a setting. Oh well, something to look into tomorrow.
