# Competitive Landscape

## What makes Focus Bear different from these apps? / If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?
In addition to app blocking, Focus Bear takes the emotional state of its users into account.

Freedom and Cold Turkey are very clinical study applications - they block distractions and provide music for focus, and that's about it. Cold Turkey in particular bills itself as being "tough" on users, which may lead to added stress, freezing up instead of working, et cetera.

Tiimo's task planning functions appear excellent, and I like the "stay focused, not frozen" motto. However, its aesthetic and language is very corporate. It feels like being monitored by a boss, not a friend.

Routiinery does not appear to have blocking functions - it seems more like a to-do list with a string of timers. (Effectively a more customisable version of Focus Bear's own Todo list). Comments on the play store also mention glitches removing their tasks and "throw[ing] my stats off", suggesting an overemphasis on getting good numbers rather than building healthy habits.

Focus Bear has more in common aesthetically with [Focus Friend](https://focusfriend.me/) - i.e. a mascot that gently encourages its users. However, Focus Friend's scope is limited to blocking apps and has no control over webpages or desktop computers.

## What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?
I like Tiimo's mood checker function. A bad mood affects productivity. Letting users acknowledge their circumstances before they start a work session - even if the app does nothing with the data - may mitigate later frustration if they don't accomplish as much as they otherwise would.

## Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?
Focus Bear overall has a positive attitude, but some aspects of it feel more punishing to users than encouraging. (e.g. a crying bear when exiting the app, leaderboards for productivity that may be disparaging to users, and the double-edged sword of having logs of your time - including incomplete sessions.) Focus Friend reframes cancelled session a little more gently, reminding users that there is always next time, and rewards users with cosmetics.

An entire customisation system would be a lot to add to Focus Bear and is likely out of scope. Still, replacing high scores and daily streaks with small improvements for existing functions (new relaxation audio, the pirate/jester/cheerleader/etc. costumes on android) may give users the same sense of progress, with none of the need to be perfect. Additionally, it may keep them coming back to see what's new.

While the core functions are good, Focus Bear is somewhat inflexible compared to its competitors. For example, it does not allow users to permanently whitelist sites that might be relevant to their work, like Freedom's does.
