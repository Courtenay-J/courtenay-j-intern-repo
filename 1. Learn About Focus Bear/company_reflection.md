# Company Reflection

## Why do you think Focus Bear was created? / What problem is Focus Bear solving?
Focus Bear was created to assist AuDHD adults focus on their work. It aims to block distractions as much as encourage good work habits, including taking breaks, eating/staying hydrated, and generally discouraging excessive focus.

While many apps will block irrelevant websites or help users organise tasks, Focus Bear additionally aims to promote peace of mind. Design challenges include the best ways to reinforce positive work/life habits and routines, determining what is relevant to a user's work vs. what should be blocked (without vilating their privacy), and how to provide encouragement and motivation/avoiding extra stress.

## Why do you think this mission is important?
Awareness around ADHD has grown considerably over the past few years. While the misconception that ADHD is only a trait observed in young boys is fading, many still do not understand the extent it impacts productivity. A person "failing" to follow organisational advice that works for neurotypicals may be misinterpreted by others as laziness or stupidity. Often, people who experience executive dysfunction will not know how to articulate what is blocking them from work, and mask the problem to avoid judgement. (This is especially common amongst women.)

Shame, however, is not a good motivator for anything.

Designing productivity tools that acknowledge these unique difficulties gives users a chance to practice good work habits - and informs them they are not alone.

## How does Focus Bear’s work align with your personal values or interests? / Do you personally relate to any of the challenges that Focus Bear aims to solve?
I am pursuing an ADHD diagnosis. (Or I will be, once that big rebate happens later this year.) As such, I have a personal interest in Focus Bear's work.

I struggled with executive dysfunction long before I became aware of the term, and most advice I was given to manage it had no effect. I am very technically minded to boot; most other blocking software was easy to impulsively bypass. I also struggle with separating work time from personal time, and am never sure if I'm working too little or too much.

In short - it is nice to be working on something that considers the humanity of its users. Some apps are so crammed full of trackers and progress reports and statistics, they feel as though they're hoping to turn people into productivity machines. Focus Bear feels like it wants to help.

I look forward to helping build healthy work environments for people who are in a similar boat.
