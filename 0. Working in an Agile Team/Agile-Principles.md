# Agile Principles
## Reflections
### What do you think are the biggest benefits and challenges of Agile?
**Benefits**
- Iterative work processes highlight issues with a design or workflow early on, as opposed to later in development
- Encourages frequent communication between team members

**Challenges**
- Reliant on team members both understanding the structure and sticking to it
- Reliant on team members feeling comfortable to communicate honestly about the project

### How can Agile principles help improve your role and responsibilities?
- Gives structure to work
- Breaks down a huge, broad task or challenge into smaller parts that may be achieved in a sprint
- Minimises chance of a small problem (be it code or workflow-based in nature) causing much larger issues further down the line

## Tasks
### Summarize the main differences between Scrum and Kanban in your own words.
Kanban is a continuous flow of work, where tasks are visualised as cards on a board. 

Kanban places limits on each group of tasks: typically how many can be in the backlog, and how many can be considered "in progress" at any given time. This encourages teams to focus their efforts, and highlights any bottlenecks. If one category of tasks has gone over its limit, it suggests there is a problem with the plan and the team will need to change their workflow.

Scrum is a series of sprints - in other words, the work stops and starts. Before a sprint, the team decides on what is to be accomplished, when it needs to be done by, and who in the team will do what. At the end of each day, the team reviews the process. At the end of the sprint period, they review the work. By analysing what went right and wrong, they can make improvements for next time. 

This constant stopping and starting keeps the plan flexible.

### Identify one Agile principle you think will be most useful in your work.
> *Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with a preference to the shorter timescale.*
> 
> -Agile Manifesto

Rigid, unchanging plans for software development projects rarely work. Developing something functional quickly and then iterating on it (including cleaning up the code, to be clear!) is vital to test the feasibility of an idea.
