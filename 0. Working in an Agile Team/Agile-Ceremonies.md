# Agile Ceremonies & Team Collaboration

## Reflection
### How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?
Agile ceremonies allow teams to plan work, set timeframes for task completion, and ensure everyone is aware of the current state of the project. Each type of ceremony has a specific purpose:
- Sprint Planning: The team decides what tasks will be worked on, the order of their completion, the team members responsible for each, and the timeframe to complete them in.
- Daily Stand-Up: Frequent, short, somewhat informal meetings to communicate progress and problems. 
- Sprint Review/Iteration Review: At the end of a sprint, the team showcases and discusses what was accomplished. Feedback is shared.
- Sprint Retrospective: The team discusses what went right during the sprint, what went wrong, and what should be improved for next time. Then, the next sprint is planned.

### Which ceremony do you think is most important for your role, and why?
For frontend development, I'd say it depends on the length of the overall project. (e.g. A sprint review or retrospective is less helpful on a tiny project with only one sprint. In longer projects, they're more important - it's vital to assess what could have gone better last time, to prevent compounding problems.) But I'd say there's a tie between the initial sprint planning and the daily stand-ups.

For sprint planning - it's vital that the team prioritise features/requests, decide which are to be completed during the sprint and when, and determine if a feature relies on another feature's completion. The stronger the plan, the smoother the sprint will (hopefully) go.

For daily stand-ups - bad news has to travel fast. If there's a blocker, the sooner the whole team knows about it the sooner they can get assistance.  (But one missed session wouldn't be as chaotic as not planning at all.)
